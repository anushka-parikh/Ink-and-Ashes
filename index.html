Ink and Ashes

A tale of love, loss, and sacrifice

After a day of the sky burning red,
He placed his gun by an empty bed.
With shaking hands, he pulled a page,
Trapped in a war, his heartbeat in a cage.

He pictured her by the window's light,
Waiting through another silent night,
Eyes on the stars, humming a tune,
As if they whispered, "He's coming home soon."

Her hair flowing in the quiet evening breeze,
Her eyes, the color of dark eternal seas.
He wrote of the days when he'd be free,
Of a life beyond war, just her and he.

He wrote of the days when the war would cease,
When she'd be in his arms, finally at peace.
The ink spilled like tears onto the pages,
Writing her promises to last through the ages.

But as he wrote the last line down,
A messenger came, with a wrinkled frown.
He spoke of a death that had come too soon
Of her, lying still beneath the moon.

With shaking hands, he rose from the chair,
The letter still there, the promises now bare.
He stepped to the battlefield, chest wide,
Inviting the bullets, no place to hide.

What good to fight a storm, now she was gone?
The reason for winning, had long been withdrawn.
No cry for mercy, no final breath,
He walked with love, into the arms of death.

As ink could not carry what lived in his chest,
The words meant for her, couldn't rest.
So he chose to deliver them, soul to soul,
Their hearts finally reuniting as one whole.

-Anushka Parikh
